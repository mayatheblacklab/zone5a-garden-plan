Design rationale: The existing woody plants define the spatial logic. Golden Juniper #2 (back-left, near house) occupies its corner and pushes back row 1 rightward; rows 2 and 3 fill in as the exclusion zone clears. Golden Juniper #1 anchors the front-left transition point. Both Creeping Junipers hold the bottom corners of the bed as low-spreading flankers, their spread extending outward beyond the bed perimeter. Every new plant center is placed outside the combined spread radius of the nearest existing anchor. Back zone: Miss Manners (M) forms a diagonal triangle across rows 2 and 3, tight against GJ#2; Catmint (B) mirrors it as a three-plant diagonal through all three rows on the right; Potentilla (A) and Echinacea (D) pair diagonally across rows 1 and 2; Sedum Autumn Joy (C) and Husker Red (L) pair diagonally across rows 2 and 3. Middle zone: Yarrow (G) runs a three-plant triangle across all three rows as a recurring gold thread; Agastache (O), Allium (H), Helenium (P), and Salvia (F) each appear as adjacent diagonal pairs; the three existing English Lavenders space evenly across row 3, with Russian Sage and Wild Lupin as singletons. Front zone: plants start to the right of GJ#1's exclusion boundary; Gaillardia (K) and Sedum Angelina (J) pair diagonally across rows 1 and 2; Dianthus (Q) repeats as two mirrored pairs for visible pink rhythm from the road; Creeping Phlox (I) brackets row 2 on both ends and sweeps row 3 as four evenly-spaced plants between the CJ corners.

Potentilla (A)

Potentilla fruticosa 'Happy Face Yellow' / 'Abbotswood'
98
Hardiness
Zone 2 — Excellent
Summer Fall Deer Resistant Zone 2–7 Blooms May–Oct

Highlights

  • 18–24 in. tall and wide; dense rounded habit
  • Blooms May through hard frost — 5+ months
  • 'Happy Face Yellow' = bright yellow; 'Abbotswood' = white
  • Extremely cold-hardy; performs in Zone 2
  • Deciduous shrub; most structural plant in this plan
  • Drought tolerant once established

Pros

  • Longest continuous bloom of any plant in this list
  • Virtually bombproof in Zone 5a — never fails to return
  • Low maintenance; annual spring cutback by one-third is all it needs
  • Tolerates poor soil, dry spells, and neglect
  • Excellent as a structural anchor at the back zone near the house

Cons

  • Slow to get going in spring — first color arrives late May
  • Bright yellow may need pairing consideration; choose 'Abbotswood' (white) for softer look
  • Can look sparse in winter without snow cover
  • Deer resistance is good but not as strong as aromatic plants

93
Hardiness
Zone 3 — Excellent

Images show Echinacea purpurea (species). 'Magnus' is the standard horticultural form and is visually representative of these photos.

Summer Early Fall Deer Resistant Zone 3–9

Highlights

  • 'Magnus': 24–30 in. tall; 'Kim's Knee High': 18–24 in. (better for this bed)
  • Rose-pink petals with raised spiny orange-bronze cone; July–September
  • North American native; exceptional pollinator and goldfinch value

  • Can self-sow prolifically if seed heads are left (thin seedlings in spring)
  • Pink tones are warm — avoid 'Magnus' if pink is a concern; try 'White Swan' instead
  • Takes 2–3 years to bloom heavily from a small pot

'Moerheim Beauty' has rich amber-red to bronze daisy blooms. Images show Helenium autumnale species; the cultivar is more deeply colored.

Late Summer Fall Deer Resistant Zone 3–8

Highlights

  • 24–36 in. tall × 18–24 in. wide; upright clumping habit
  • Rich amber-bronze to burnt-orange daisy flowers, July–September
  • North American native; exceptional pollinator and butterfly value
  • Bitter foliage reliably avoided by deer
  • Pairs with Sedum Autumn Joy for a long warm-season finale

Pros

  • Warm amber-orange color provides the best fall contrast to the purple-blue wave
  • Zone 3 hardy; no winter protection needed in Eastern Canada
  • Deadheading extends bloom window significantly
  • Good vertical presence at the middle zone; adds height without overwhelming

Cons

  • Prefers consistent moisture — a dry south-facing bed is its biggest challenge; water during drought
  • Can become leggy without pinching in early June (Chelsea chop)
  • Needs division every 3 years to prevent center die-out
  • Foliage can wilt dramatically in heat before roots establish

Dianthus gratianopolitanus 'Firewitch'

'Firewitch' (Feuerhexe) is a hot-pink cultivar of the cheddar pink. Images show Dianthus gratianopolitanus species; the cultivar has deeply magenta-pink blooms with a spicy clove scent.

Spring Early Summer Reblooms Evergreen Foliage Deer Resistant Zone 3–9

Highlights

  • 6–10 in. tall × 12–18 in. wide; tight silver-blue evergreen mat
  • Vivid magenta-pink fringed flowers, May–June; reblooms lightly after deadheading
  • 2006 Perennial Plant of the Year
  • Strongly clove-scented flowers — aromatic deterrent to deer
  • Evergreen silver-blue foliage provides year-round contrast against dark mulch

Pros

  • Silver-blue foliage is a unique year-round texture in the front zone alongside Creeping Phlox
  • Provides the only strong pink note in the plan — a vivid color accent from street view
  • Zone 3 hardy; extremely reliable in Eastern Canada
  • Drought tolerant and heat tolerant; ideal for the south-facing dry front zone
  • Low and tidy — never needs staking or cutting back

Cons

  • Main bloom is 3–4 weeks; rebloom after deadheading is lighter
  • Needs excellent drainage — will rot in wet clay or soggy mulch
  • Silver foliage can bleach to grey in heavy shade; full sun mandatory for best color
  • Center can die out after 4–5 years; divide and replant outer sections
