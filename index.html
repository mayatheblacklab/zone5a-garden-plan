Planting Plan

House / Arborvitae backdrop — top edge 2.42 m (existing)
Curb / Street Edge — front edge 4.26 m (widest point)
Design rationale: The bed narrows toward the house (2.42 m) and opens toward the curb (4.26 m), so plant density is highest at the back and spreads outward. The back zone packs all 9 tall plants into 3 staggered rows across the narrow top strip, alternating colour deliberately so purple Catmint, mauve Echinacea, and earthy Sedum Autumn Joy never sit adjacent. The middle zone exploits the widening space with a 4-plant row at its centre, interleaving yellow Yarrow plates against blue-purple Salvia and Allium globes. The front zone uses the full curb width: Golden Juniper (existing) anchors the left corner, Lavender and Blue Star Juniper bookend the wide first row, Sedum Angelina fills the middle row with year-round chartreuse foliage, and Creeping Phlox carpets the entire front edge as a spring-burst weed suppressor.

Plant Guide

Potentilla (A)

Potentilla fruticosa 'Happy Face Yellow' / 'Abbotswood'
98
Hardiness
Zone 2 — Excellent
Summer Fall Deer Resistant Zone 2–7 Blooms May–Oct

Highlights

  • 18–24 in. tall and wide; dense rounded habit
  • Blooms May through hard frost — 5+ months
  • 'Happy Face Yellow' = bright yellow; 'Abbotswood' = white
  • Extremely cold-hardy; performs in Zone 2
  • Deciduous shrub; most structural plant in this plan
  • Drought tolerant once established

Pros

  • Longest continuous bloom of any plant in this list
  • Virtually bombproof in Zone 5a — never fails to return
  • Low maintenance; annual spring cutback by one-third is all it needs
  • Tolerates poor soil, dry spells, and neglect
  • Excellent as a structural anchor flanking the arborvitaes

Cons

  • Slow to get going in spring — first color arrives late May
  • Bright yellow may need pairing consideration; choose 'Abbotswood' (white) for softer look
  • Can look sparse in winter without snow cover
  • Deer resistance is good but not as strong as aromatic plants

Echinacea 'Magnus' (D)

Echinacea purpurea 'Magnus' — or 'Kim's Knee High' for compact form
93
Hardiness
Zone 3 — Excellent

Images show Echinacea purpurea (species). 'Magnus' is the standard horticultural form and is visually representative of these photos.

Summer Early Fall Deer Resistant Zone 3–9

Highlights

  • 'Magnus': 24–30 in. tall; 'Kim's Knee High': 18–24 in. (better for this bed)
  • Rose-pink petals with raised spiny orange-bronze cone; July–September
  • North American native; exceptional pollinator and goldfinch value
  • Coarse, rough-textured foliage; generally deer-avoided
  • Seed heads provide winter structure and bird food if left standing

Pros

  • Bold, showy flowers in the mid-summer gap between salvia and sedum
  • Native plant — better ecological value than ornamentals
  • Attracts goldfinches to seed heads in fall and winter
  • Tolerates poor, dry soil; thrives in south-facing heat
  • Long-lived once established; rarely needs dividing

Cons

  • Deer resistance is good but not as reliable as aromatic plants
  • Can self-sow prolifically if seed heads are left (thin seedlings in spring)
  • Pink tones are warm — avoid 'Magnus' if pink is a concern; try 'White Swan' instead
  • Takes 2–3 years to bloom heavily from a small pot

Russian Sage 'Little Spire' (E)

Salvia yangii 'Little Spire' (formerly Perovskia atriplicifolia)
72
Hardiness
Zone 5 — Good
Mid Summer Fall Deer Resistant Zone 5–10

Highlights

  • 24 in. tall and wide; upright, airy silver-white branching stems
  • Violet-blue flowers July through October — one of the longest bloomers
  • Pungent aromatic foliage; deer avoid it consistently
  • Silver stems provide winter structure after foliage drops
  • 'Little Spire' is the compact form; standard Perovskia reaches 4+ ft

Pros

  • Violet-blue from July to frost — fills the critical late-season color gap
  • Airy, fine texture contrasts beautifully with bold-leaved plants
  • Extremely drought tolerant; south-facing heat is not a problem
  • Silver-white stems remain ornamental in snow

Cons

  • Zone 5 is its cold limit — lower hardiness score than most plants here
  • Must NOT be cut back in fall; old stems protect the crown through winter
  • Needs excellent drainage; will not survive wet clay over winter
  • Woody base dies back fully; looks dead until late May — do not pull it out

Yarrow 'Moonshine' (G)

Achillea × 'Moonshine'
96
Hardiness
Zone 3 — Excellent

Image 1 is 'Moonshine' cultivar. Images 2–3 show Achillea millefolium (species) as representative of the same flat-top habit and silvery foliage.

Summer Reblooms Deer Resistant Zone 3–9

Highlights

  • 18–24 in. tall and wide; flat-topped flower clusters on upright stems
  • Canary yellow clusters June–August; fades to soft cream as it ages
  • Silvery-gray feathery foliage is ornamental even outside bloom
  • Strongly aromatic; one of the most deer-avoided perennials
  • 'Moonshine' is a sterile hybrid — less aggressive than species yarrow

Pros

  • Bold color contrast to the blue/purple salvia and catmint in the middle zone
  • Silver foliage provides interest between bloom flushes
  • Extremely drought tolerant; thrives in poor, dry soil
  • Very cold-hardy and long-lived
  • Deadhead for a second flush in late summer

Cons

  • Rich or moist soil causes floppy stems that need staking
  • Spreads by rhizome and can invade neighbors; divide every 3–4 years
  • Cut spent flower clusters promptly or they look untidy
  • Bright yellow may be strong against certain house colors — consider pairing first

Mid Summer Strong Deer Deterrent Zone 4–9

Highlights

  • 15–20 in. tall × 10–15 in. wide; upright clumping habit
  • Lavender-pink globe flowers July–August
  • 2018 Perennial Plant of the Year

  • Allium scent is one of the most reliable deer deterrents in horticulture

Pros

  • Acts as a living deer repellent scattered through the middle zone

  • No deadheading required

Cons

  • Relatively narrow clumping habit — less visual mass than spreading plants
  • Foliage dies back completely in winter; crown slow to emerge in spring
  • Needs dividing every 3–4 years to maintain vigor
  • Bloom window is 4–6 weeks — shorter than most other plants in this list

Lavender 'Hidcote' (I)

Lavandula angustifolia 'Hidcote'
67
Hardiness
Zone 5 — Marginal
Summer Semi-Evergreen Deer Resistant Zone 5–8

Highlights

  • 10–18 in. tall × 18–24 in. wide; tidy mounded habit
  • Dark purple flower spikes June–July
  • Semi-evergreen silver-gray foliage; provides off-season structure
  • South-facing full-sun exposure is the ideal microclimate for Zone 5a
  • Strongly aromatic; one of the most deer-avoided plants in any garden

Pros

  • Evergreen structure and silver foliage visible from the street year-round
  • Your south-facing bed maximizes its chances of overwintering
  • Compact 'Hidcote' is better-suited than larger varieties for front edging
  • Excellent deer deterrent — strongly aromatic
  • Pairs beautifully with the dark mulch; silver foliage pops

Cons

  • Lowest hardiness score in this plan — Zone 5 is its limit
  • Crown rot from wet mulch kills more lavender than cold; keep mulch away from the stem
  • Cannot cut into old wood — only trim by one-third in early spring
  • May partially die back after a harsh winter; assess in May before assuming dead
  • Needs excellent drainage — amend with sand/gravel if soil is heavy

Creeping Phlox (J)

Phlox subulata — 'Emerald Blue', 'Purple Beauty', or 'White Delight'
95
Hardiness
Zone 3 — Excellent
Spring Evergreen Deer Resistant Zone 3–9

Highlights

  • 4–6 in. tall × 18–24 in. wide; dense evergreen mat
  • Covered in blue, purple, or white flowers April–May (3–4 weeks)
  • Evergreen needle-like foliage provides year-round ground cover
  • Cascades beautifully over the curb edge
  • Excellent for suppressing weeds in the front zone

Pros

  • The only plant in this list that covers the front zone completely in spring bloom
  • Evergreen mat looks clean and manicured against dark mulch all year
  • Cold-hardy to Zone 3 — never an issue in Eastern Canada
  • Cascades over the curb for a polished, finished edge
  • Highly affordable; 4 plants cover the front strip well

Cons

  • Bloom period is short — 3 to 4 weeks only; no rebloom
  • Needs light shearing by one-third after bloom to prevent woodiness
  • Can get leggy and woody in the center after 4–5 years; divide to refresh
  • Avoid pink-red cultivars to stay within the no-red palette

Year-Round Foliage Summer Flowers Fall Color Shift Deer Resistant Zone 3–9

Blue Star Juniper (L)

Juniperus squamata 'Blue Star'
87
Hardiness
Zone 4 — Very Good
Year-Round Structure Deer Resistant Zone 4–9

Highlights

  • Slow growing; stays under 2 ft for many years, eventually 24–36 in.
  • Steel-blue needle foliage year-round — unique color in this planting
  • Prickly, resinous needles deter deer extremely effectively
  • Perfectly rounded mounding habit requires no pruning
  • Provides four-season structure as front-right anchor

Pros

  • Steel-blue is a striking color contrast against the warm-toned perennials
  • No maintenance — symmetrical and self-shaping

  • Year-round anchor point at the front-right corner of the bed

Cons

  • Very slow — starts small and takes years to make an impact
  • Will eventually exceed 2.5 ft, but this is many years away
  • Needs excellent drainage; susceptible to root rot in wet clay
  • Prickly texture makes weeding around it slightly awkward
